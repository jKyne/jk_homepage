Pipeline for lumen/Wall separation project.

Disease Diagnosis: Lumen/Wall Volume Ratio Measurement on Pulmonary Vessels with CT Scans

Date:
2020.03-2020.06
Advisor:
Prof. Andrew Laine & Prof. R. Graham Barr
Site link:
https://hbil.bme.columbia.edu/

This project aims to help lung disease diagnosis through automatically quantifying the lumen/wall ratio on pulmonary vessels. Since clinically, given the same total pulmonary vascular volume, different lumen/wall volume ratios may reveal different pathologies. To measure this ratio value, we segmented and separate the lumen and wall of vessels based on the enhanced CT scans. And in the future, we want to transfer this pipeline to adapt for….., because non-contrast CT request lower radiation dose thus will reduce the harm to the patients.

Pipeline for probe tracking project

Image-guided Surgery: Object Tracking & Pose Estimation In VR/AR Surgeries

Date:
2018.06-2019.06
Advisor:
Prof. Hongkai Xiong
Site link:
http://min.sjtu.edu.cn/

This project aims to develop an object tracking and pose estimation method for a VR/AR surgery. This pipeline can be divided into two parts, the first part tracks the object within the 2D space of videos, and the second part estimated the pose of the object in 3D space to guide the operation for doctors. I revised the YOLO algorithm, in order to track the corner and centroid of our object. And with the corner coordinates, I changed them into a sparse representation, and with a duall dictionary learning method, I can get the pose estimation of the object.
